What is StringBean?

The featherweight responsive CSS Framework based on a 16-point system (rather than the traditional 12-point system that other frameworks use) this gives the developer the power to divide the screen up in more finite segments. The extra 4 break points give you greater control over the widths of content on your site. Sometimes, 12 is just too few, especially on a high resolution screen, such as 4K - at 4K String Bean comes into its own!

In addition to this, String Bean also has 5 breakpoints instead of the traditional 4. You can implement your design with: xsmall, small, medium, large, and xlarge.

How does it work?

Let's have a look at some of the things you can do with the String Bean framework...

Forms & Inputs

  • Send Request

    • Forms use the same breakpoint classes that are used on the other elements, except you use a Grid and "li" elements to construct it. Like so :- 

    <p class="grid">
    <form action="" method="post">
    
        <li class="box xsmall-6 small-4 medium-4 large-2 xlarge-2">
            <label>
                First Name:
            </label>
        </li>
        
        <li class="box xsmall-16 small-12 medium-12 large-14 xlarge-14">
            <input type="text" value="Enter your first name..." class="full-16" />
        </li>
        
        <li class="box xsmall-16 small-4 medium-4 large-2 xlarge-2">
            <label>
                Email Address:
            </label>
        </li>
        
        <li class="box xsmall-16 small-12 medium-12 large-14 xlarge-14">
            <input type="text" value="Enter your email address..." class="full-16" />
        </li>
        
        <li class="box xsmall-16 small-16 medium-16 large-16 xlarge-16 align-right">
            <input type="submit" value="Send Request" class="button information" />
        </li>
        
    </form>
</p>

    Hiding content below breakpoints

    You can use the hide-below-x (where x is the xsmall, small, medium, large, or xlarge breakpoints) to hide content. This section is tagged as such, so you won't be able to see it below "large". 

    <div class="grid hide-below-medium">
    <li class="box xsmall-16 small-16 medium-8 large-4 xlarge-4">
        <img src="img/AdvertisementForLargeScreens.jpg" />
    </li>
</div>
